Data protection information

We thank you for our interest in our Study “A UniversalWeb Accessibility Feedback Form”. We study the use of a feedback tool for web accessibility. We want to understand how people interact with the feedback tool and the resulting feedback. In the following, we provide information about data protection.

1. Personal Data

Within the scope of your participation in the above study, we will be processing personal data of the participants. According to Art. 4 Nr. 1 of the European GDPR, ‘personal data’ means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.

Within this study, these are the following pieces of information concerning you:

  • Description of the feedback
  • Assistive technologies and adaptive strategies
  • Browser, operating system
  • Files like screenshots
  • Name, email, phone

3. Purpose of data processing

KIT processes your personal data solely for specified, unambiguous and legitimate purposes. The purpose of the data processing in the present is to carry out the study “Designing a Feedback Tool for Web Accessibility”.

4. Legal basis for data processing

The legal basis for the processing of your personal data is your consent in accordance with Art. 6, Abs. 1 a) of GDPR.
Consent is granted voluntarily. Consent can be revoked at any time with the effect for the future. Effect for the future means that a withdrawal of consent does not affect the legality of the processing carried out based on the consent until the revocation. If consent is refused or revoked, there are no disadvantages.

5. Description of data processing

Users can fill out the feedback form. After clicking the submit button, the data will be transferred. If the user wishes to receive a copy of the feedback by email, the email address provided will only be used for this purpose. To evaluate the feedback tool for the study, the transferred feedback is used. All data collected during the study will be strictly confidentially and stored, further processed, and evaluated and will only be shared with the research partners at KIT in pseudonymised form. The dataset will be given a randomized ID number. This ensures that no conclusions about you are possible. The table containing the feedback data will be deleted immediately after the completion of the study, so that no assignment to your person is possible anymore. Your name, email, and phone are then removed from the research data. The anonymized research data will be kept for research and may be published in a report or journal article or presented to interested parties, including possibly, blog article but not exclusively, members of editorial boards or scientific committees. The results of the study are published exclusively in anonymized and/or aggregated form.

Participation of the study is voluntary. You will not be at a disadvantage if you do not decide to participate in the study. Even if you have given your consent to participate, you can cancel your participation at any time, with or without giving reasons. If you wish, the collected data will be deleted in full or in part. This is only possible until the deletion of your name, email, and phone from the research data. Subsequently, the data can no longer be assigned to individual persons.

6. Recipient

In principle, your personal data will not be transmitted to third parties.
If it is exceptionally necessary for external service providers to process personal data for us on behalf of us, these are carefully selected by us and contractually obliged. The respective service providers will work exclusively according to our instructions. We ensure this through strict contractual regulations, technical and organisational measures and additional controls. There is no transfer of personal data to third countries outside the EU or the EEA or to an international organisation. Automated decision-making, including profiling, is not performed.

7. Storage time

The name, email, and phone will be deleted immediately after completing the evaluation. The anonymized research data will be kept for research and might be published in a report, blog article or journal article.

8. Rights

You have the following rights in relation to personal data concerning you:

  • the right to confirm whether data concerning you is processed and to obtain information about the data processed, to further information on data processing and to copies of the data (Art. 15 GDPR),
  • the right to correct or complete inaccurate or incomplete data (Art. 16 GDPR),
  • right to immediate deletion of the data concerning you (Art. 17 GDPR),
  • right to restrict processing (Article 18 GDPR),
  • right to receive the data concerning you and provided by you and to transfer this data to other controllers (Art. 20 GDPR).
